Aim Overview

Course Outcomes: Searching (Linear and Binary)

  • Understand the Concept of Binary Search:Students will be able to explain the principles of binary search, understand how it works by dividing a sorted data set in half repeatedly, and recognize its importance in efficiently retrieving data from large, sorted collections.
  • Apply Binary Search Technique: Students will demonstrate the ability to implement the binary search algorithm, both iteratively and recursively, and understand when it is applicable, particularly when working with sorted data sets. They will also be able to compare its application against linear search in different scenarios.
  • Analyze Binary Search Efficiency: Students will evaluate the efficiency of binary search in terms of time complexity (O(log n)) and space complexity, and understand its significant advantage over linear search for larger, sorted data sets. Additionally, they will analyze scenarios where binary search is not applicable, such as when the data is unsorted.
  • Understand Real-world Applications of Binary Search: Students will recognize and explain real-world applications of binary search algorithms, such as searching for a value in large, sorted databases, looking up entries in phone directories or dictionaries, or optimizing algorithms in software systems where the data is pre-ordered or easily sorted.
