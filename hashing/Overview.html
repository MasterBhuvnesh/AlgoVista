Prerequisites and Module Overview


Prerequisites:

  • Basic Programming Skills:Students should be proficient in at least one programming language (e.g., Python, C++, Java) and understand fundamental programming concepts such as loops, conditionals, and functions.
  • Data Structures: Knowledge of basic data structures like arrays, linked lists, stacks, and queues is essential, as hashing often involves manipulating these structures
  • Mathematical Foundations: Understanding of discrete mathematics, particularly modular arithmetic and probability, which are often used in designing and analyzing hash functions.
  • Problem-Solving Skills: Strong analytical and problem-solving skills to approach and solve complex problems, which is essential when dealing with collisions and optimizing hash functions.

Module Overview:

This module on data structures provides an in-depth exploration of various data structures. It includes:

  • Lectures: Comprehensive lessons covering the theory and concepts of data structures, including arrays, lists, and matrices.
  • Hands-On Practice Exercises: Practical activities designed to reinforce your understanding and application of the concepts.
  • Quizzes for Self-Assessment: Interactive quizzes to evaluate your progress and solidify your knowledge.

Throughout this module, you will gain valuable skills and insights into the effective use of arrays, lists, and matrices, preparing you for more advanced topics in data structures and algorithms.
